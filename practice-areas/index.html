Practice Area 01

Workers'
Compensation

We understand the California workers' compensation system and know how to help you navigate the process of seeking your rightful benefits. When you work with our firm, you get an entire team of legal professionals working for you.

In California, all workers designated as employees are eligible for workers' compensation — regardless of whether they work full-time, part-time, or seasonally. It does not matter if your employer has 100 employees or just one; nearly all California employers are required by law to carry workers' compensation insurance.

If you were injured while performing work duties — even if you were partially at fault — you may be entitled to benefits. California's workers' compensation system is a no-fault system, which means you don't need to prove your employer was negligent.

You must report your injury or illness to your employer in writing within 30 days of the incident. For a work-related illness, report it in writing within 30 days of missing work or seeing a doctor and learning the condition is work-related.

After reporting, your employer must provide you with a claim form (DWC-1) within one business day. You complete the employee section and return it — your employer then files it with their insurance carrier. From there, the insurer has 90 days to accept or deny your claim.

This is where having an attorney matters. A missed deadline or incomplete form can jeopardize your entire claim. We help you get it right from the start.

Workers' compensation claims are rarely straightforward. Employers and insurers routinely dispute, delay, or deny legitimate claims. Common problems include:

  • Employers disputing that an injury is work-related
  • Insurers denying benefits outright or underpaying
  • Treating physicians releasing you to work before you have fully healed
  • Employers failing to offer light-duty or modified work accommodations
  • Pressure to return to work before recovery is complete

Having an experienced attorney levels the playing field. We know the tactics insurers use — and how to counter them. We handle all communication with the insurance company so you can focus on recovering.

California workers' compensation provides several categories of benefits:

  • Medical Care: Reasonable and necessary medical treatment related to your work injury, paid for by your employer's insurer
  • Temporary Disability Indemnity: Wage replacement while you are off work due to your injury
  • Permanent Disability Indemnity: Compensation once your injury stabilizes and results in lasting impairment
  • Supplemental Job Displacement Benefit: Voucher for retraining if you cannot return to your previous job
  • Return-to-Work Indemnity: Additional support if your employer cannot accommodate your work restrictions
  • Death Benefits: Payable to dependents in the event of a fatal work injury

Workers' compensation attorney fees in California are set by law at 12%–15% of any settlement or award, and must be approved by a workers' compensation judge. You pay nothing unless we win your case.

There are no court costs, no upfront retainer, and no hidden fees. Payment comes directly from the insurance company's settlement or award — not from your pocket.

No Win, No Fee — Guaranteed. If we don't recover compensation for you, you owe us nothing.
